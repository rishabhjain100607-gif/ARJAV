01 - Candidate Note AASHA
A Note from the Candidate

Why AASHA
matters

Arjav Jain Candidate

AASHA has always been the conscience of our campus.

It is one of those rare committees where the work actually matters. But I believe we can do even more. After understanding AASHA's journey, events, reach, and challenges, I see potential waiting to be fully unlocked.

My focus for the year ahead is simple: make AASHA more intentional. Every event should have a clear purpose, a measurable outcome, and enough structure to earn the approvals, timelines, and trust it needs.

01 Purpose Every initiative begins with a real reason to exist.
02 Structure Roles, timelines, and proposals stay visible.
03 Community The work should be felt inside campus and beyond it.

03 - Internal Community For our people
Community Lab

For Our
People

Programs that make students feel welcomed, connected, and guided through the real rhythms of campus life.

01

El Amigo

Revamped

A buddy-style peer connection initiative pairing students across batches to build mentorship and belonging from day one.

New this year: structured check-in milestones across the semester so connections grow beyond the first meeting.
02

Buddy Project

New

A relaxed, game-based interaction when the new batch arrives, helping juniors meet seniors without the stiffness of formal orientation.

Design shift: interest-based grouping by hobbies, hometowns, and career tracks to make conversations natural.
03

Swagatam

Continuing

The flagship welcome initiative for incoming batches, helping students feel seen, supported, and part of something meaningful.

Addition: an informal Life at IPM session with honest senior-led conversations about campus culture.

04 - Internal Community Growing together
Internal Community continued

Growing
Together

More spaces for students to think, compete, give, and build meaningful connections with causes beyond themselves.

04

Case Competitions

Revamped

Social-impact case competitions giving students a platform to think critically about real-world problems. Impetus remains the anchor competition on policy and sustainability.

New this year: explore collaboration with Indore Municipal Corporation for bigger prize pools, wider participation, and real civic problem statements.
05

STEM Quiz

New

A campus-wide quiz across social sciences, technology, economics, and mathematics to encourage interdisciplinary thinking.

What's different: a Social Impact Round where teams solve real-world case problems under time pressure.
06

NGO Donation Drive

New

A campus-wide drive for a partnered NGO, collecting essentials, funds, or time while opening a live project pathway for interested students.

The bigger idea: make AASHA a bridge between IPM students and NGOs looking for young talent.

Internal Community continued

Creativity
and Action

Events that turn expression, fitness, and campus energy into visible social purpose.

07

Impact Palette

Revamped

Art meets social impact through a creative expression event where students use visual storytelling to communicate social issues they care about.

Runsday Marathon

A campus-wide run combining fitness with social purpose and collective responsibility, already one of AASHA's most loved recurring events.

06 - External Community Beyond campus
External Community

Reaching
Beyond Us

Initiatives that extend AASHA's impact to the communities, individuals, and causes that need us most.

01

Aabhar Utsav

Revamped

A heartfelt evening celebrating the housekeeping and security workers who keep campus running every day.

New this year: Faces of IPM, a photo documentation series sharing worker stories on AASHA platforms.
02

Samvedna

Continuing

Visits to schools and centres for differently-abled children, creating moments of joy, play, and genuine human connection.

Upgrade: increase to two visits per semester with local NGO support for better-tailored activities.
03

Panacea

Revamped

An online problem-solving challenge where students propose socially conscious solutions to India-facing issues.

New this year: top solutions presented to a faculty jury, with selected ideas considered for NGO collaboration.

07 - External Community Every person matters
External Community continued

Every Person
Matters

Simple, consistent gestures can become lasting institutional habits when they are planned well.

04

Guard Gratitude Day

New

A meaningful initiative for the security guards who watch over campus day and night, pairing refreshments with handwritten notes of thanks.

Measurable change: document reach across campus posts and explore making this a fixed annual AASHA event.

NIOS Outreach

Restarted

Restart the initiative where seniors tutor elderly community members preparing for Class 10 examinations through NIOS.

New this year: monthly learner check-ins, volunteer sign-ups, and a celebration when learners clear exams.

AASHA Broadcast

Why it matters: direct communication means more people show up and more hands join the work.

08 - Structure How we operate
Structure and Accountability

How We
Operate

A well-run committee earns the right to do bigger things. The goal is a process that builds trust from the inside out.

01 Written proposal Event objective, audience, budget, venue, resources, and timeline.
02 Review and approval Feasibility, permissions, risk checks, and final sign-off.
03 Execution Owners, checklist, communications, documentation, and reflection.
Chair / Secretary Strategy, external representation, and final sign-offs.
Events Lead End-to-end planning, logistics, and execution.
Outreach Lead NGO coordination and community engagement.
Content and Docs Social media, documentation, and committee visibility.
Collaboration goals

Partner with at least two student committees, forge three or more NGO or organisation partnerships, and invite faculty as jury members or advisors for Panacea and Case Competitions.

Proposal checklist
Event name, objective, and intended audience Estimated budget, venue, and resources needed Timeline and key milestones before execution
